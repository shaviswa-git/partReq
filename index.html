The Feature

From reorder alert to reconciled ledgers — without the manual handoffs.

Today

Parts requisitions at automotive OEMs travel through a dozen inboxes before goods move.

A dealer emails a reorder request. An inside-sales rep keys it into the order system. A planner eyeballs inventory and decides whether to ship from stock or buy. A buyer raises the PO. A billing clerk raises the invoice. AP waits on a paper supplier invoice to match manually. Errors compound, lead times slip, and finance closes late.

With Axle

A coordinated group of agents owns the workflow end-to-end, with clear systems of record at every step.

A reorder-watching agent drafts the request. A processor agent triages, prices, and routes it. A procurement agent sources when inventory is short. An AR agent bills the dealer the moment the shipment ships. An AP agent 3-way matches the supplier invoice. Humans stay in the loop for consent and proof of delivery — nothing else.

The Cast

Five agents. One clean handoff pipeline.

Each agent owns a narrow responsibility with a clear input, a clear output, and a single system of record it writes to. No agent is a general-purpose AI — they are purpose-built components assembled into a flow.

What the demo shows

Built to answer the hard questions your stakeholders will ask.

Every click maps to a real system-of-record interaction. This is not a slideware flow — it is a working interaction model for how the feature should behave in production.

01 Two pricing sources, clearly separated

Dealer invoices are always priced from the dealer price list. Supplier POs are always priced from the supplier contract. The demo makes this visible in every scenario so pricing governance stays clean.

02 System-of-record transparency

Every record the flow creates — Parts Request, Shipment Line, PO, Shipment, AR invoice, AP invoice — is clickable, opens an ERP-style record page, and shows the posting, audit trail, and line detail.

03 Automated 3-way match

The AP agent reconciles the supplier invoice against the PO and the receipt (GRN or dealer POD). Matches post straight to AP with no human approval in the happy path.

04 Flexible fulfillment routing

Procurement chooses between OEM warehouse routing (for consolidation) and direct drop-ship (for speed) based on contract clauses, dealer urgency, and geographic fit — modeled transparently.

05 Activity log & audit

Every agent and system action is timestamped in an activity pane. The audit trail on each record makes it clear which agent did what and when — essential for finance sign-off.

06 Minimal human loop

Only two human touchpoints: the dealer tapping Order parts to consent to spend, and the proof-of-delivery confirmation on receipt. Everything else runs between agents.
